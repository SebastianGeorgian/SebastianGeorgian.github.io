What carried over

None of this is engineering, and I'm not going to pretend it is. But a summer season on the coast teaches a few things that hold up anywhere.

  • Working under load. A full restaurant on an August evening doesn't care how you feel about it. You work the queue, in order, until it's gone.
  • Showing up. Seasonal work is unforgiving about reliability — the shift starts whether you're ready or not, and the next person depends on the state you leave things in.
  • Dealing with people directly. Service work is a long lesson in staying civil and clear with someone who is unhappy — which turns out to be most of communication.
  • Doing it while studying. The degree, the thesis project and these shifts overlapped. That was the point.
